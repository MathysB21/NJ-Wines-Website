About Us

Klein Simonsvlei was established by Piet Joubert, who acquired the property in 1898 with the main focus of cultivating fruit. His son Daniel joined him at the farm when he was 22 years old, and later Niel, Daniel's son, took over the management in 1950. Although some grapes were grown at the beginning of the century, production remained small until the fourth generation, when Daan assumed full control in 1981.

The family plays a significant role in the current operations. Daan leads the team, serving as both the viticulturist and fruit farmer. Niel, who is a qualified winemaker, manages the financial aspects and supports Daan in farming activities. Mari, their sister, handles sales, distribution, and social initiatives such as a staff children's crèche and an after-school club through the Pebbles Project.
Eleanor, Daan's wife, provides assistance whenever necessary and plays a vital supportive role. Christine-Marié, Daan's mother, resides on the property in a beautiful garden setting and takes care of the gardens.
